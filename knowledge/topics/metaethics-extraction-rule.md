## Proposed rule

A text dense with confident first-order moral claims ("slavery is evil",
"cruelty is always wrong") provides no evidence for realist ultimate_reality or
constituents claims, because quasi-realist expressivism (Blackburn) reproduces
the whole of first-order moral discourse — including most "it would be wrong
even if we approved" constructions — without realist metaphysics. Only
explicitly metaethical spans license extraction: assertions of
mind-independence, moral discovery or moral knowledge framed against possible
collective error, comparisons of moral to mathematical or scientific truth.
