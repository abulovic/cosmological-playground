## Scope and variants

Moral realism = cognitivism + some moral truths + mind-independence. Two live
secular variants: robust non-naturalism (Moore, Ross, Parfit, Enoch, Scanlon,
Wielenberg — irreducibly normative facts) and naturalist realism (Cornell
realism: Boyd, Sturgeon, Brink; reductive: Railton — moral facts are natural
facts). Historically the strain lived inside full cosmologies: Plato's Form of
the Good, the Stoic logos, natural-law theism, Kant's postulates.

## Substantive commitments (5 of 12 primitives)

Ultimate reality and constituents: moral facts and properties exist
stance-independently; robust realism adds irreducibly normative facts to
reality's inventory while naturalism adds nothing beyond the natural. Causality
and agency: the load-bearing split — causally inert moral facts (robust) versus
causally efficacious ones (Cornell: depravity explains atrocity). Human place:
humans are discoverers of value, not its authors; the normative order binds any
rational agent. Epistemic access: forced by the causality choice — intuition
and a-priori access (robust, exposed to Street's evolutionary debunking and the
Benacerraf–Field reliability challenge) versus empirical access (naturalist,
exposed to Moore's open question).

## Silences

Silent on origin, cosmic structure, time, self and consciousness, fundamental
problem, transformation, destiny. Pointed silences: realism supplies the
precondition for objective moral error (fundamental_problem) and moral progress
as discovery (transformation) without asserting either; modern secular realism
refuses the Sidgwick/Kant destiny pressure and accepts an objective normative
order that nothing in the cosmos enforces.

## Card-deck consequences

"Moral realism" is a navigation category, not a card: the variants diverge on
4 of their 5 substantive primitives, so representation needs two scoped cards
(robust non-naturalist realism; Cornell naturalist realism). The existing deck
already distinguishes them: Cornell realism nests compatibly inside
scientific-naturalism; robust non-naturalism contradicts its "everything is
physical" ultimate_reality claim. Candidate provenance for curation: SEP "Moral
Realism", "Moral Non-Naturalism", "Moral Naturalism", "Moral Anti-Realism"
(unverified as of 2026-07-06).
