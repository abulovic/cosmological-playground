## The concept

A doctrine is cosmologically orphaned when it retains a commitment that
historically depended on a fuller cosmology while dropping the supporting
primitives. Paradigm case: modern secular moral realism keeps an objective
normative order but is silent on origin, structure, and destiny — the
primitives through which Plato (Form of the Good), Stoicism (logos), natural-law
theism (creation and judgment), and Kant (postulated God and immortality) once
supported it.

## Why it is analytically useful

Orphaning turns a debate about a position's viability into a dependency graph
over primitives. Street's evolutionary debunking argument is an orphaning
complaint: a naturalistic origin story for our faculties plus causally inert
moral facts leaves the epistemic-access claim unaccountable. The theistic moral
argument runs the inference in reverse — from the orphaned profile back to the
bundled cosmology. Expect the pattern elsewhere: secularized human dignity
(imago Dei without the imago), progress narratives (providence without a
provider), karma-like desert intuitions without rebirth cosmology.

## Detection heuristic

Profile the doctrine across the twelve primitives; an orphaning candidate shows
a strong commitment on one primitive whose historical carriers all made
supporting claims on primitives where the modern version is silent. The silence
pattern, not the commitment itself, is the diagnostic.
