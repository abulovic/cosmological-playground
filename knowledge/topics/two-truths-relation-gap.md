## The gap

The frozen relation vocabulary (entails / compatible / unspecified / tension /
contradicts) has no way to say "contradicts ultimately, compatible
conventionally". Madhyamaka exposes it: karma functions as a robustly objective
moral order at the conventional level while moral facts with own-nature
(svabhāva) are denied ultimately. A single relation per claim pair forces a
false choice between tension and compatible.

## Where it bites

Any two-truths or levels-of-description tradition at 24-card scale: Madhyamaka
and Yogācāra explicitly; Advaita Vedānta (vyāvahārika/pāramārthika) exactly
parallel; arguably scientific images vs manifest images in Sellarsian
naturalism. Also re-triggered by the Yudkowsky dry run's gap #1 (rival
non-exclusive answers to the same primitive).

## Cheapest fix

A card-schema note before D2-001 curates any two-truths tradition: allow a
claim to carry an optional level qualifier (e.g. conventional/ultimate) so
comparison can pair claims level-to-level instead of flattening them. Relation
vocabulary itself stays frozen.
