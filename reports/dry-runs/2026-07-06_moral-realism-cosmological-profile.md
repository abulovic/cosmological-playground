# Doctrine profile: moral realism across the twelve cosmological primitives

- Date: 2026-07-06
- Mode: **doctrine profiling** (card-drafting mode), not text analysis
- Contracts applied: `docs/COSMOLOGY_PRIMITIVES.md` v1.0.0; `docs/CONFIDENCE_AND_RANKING.md` v1.0.0 (relation vocabulary only)
- Status: analytic exercise from general philosophical knowledge. **Not a card.** No claim here has verified scholarly provenance; promotion to cards under D2-001 requires sourced citations and the review-needed flag.

## 0. Method notes

The frozen contract defines evidence states (`explicit` … `unobserved`) for *claims made by textual voices*. A philosophical strain is not a text: it has no spans, no voices, no stances. Profiling it requires an analogous but distinct scale, used only in this report:

| Doctrinal status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `constitutive` | Part of what makes a position count as moral realism at all |
| `characteristic` | Held across essentially all variants, but derivable rather than definitional |
| `variant-dependent` | The major variants give incompatible answers |
| `peripheral` | Surfaces only under philosophical pressure, or as historical association |
| `silent` | The strain qua strain asserts nothing on this primitive |

`silent` is the doctrinal analog of the contract's rule 8: absence is `unspecified`, never `contradicts`.

**Scope definition used.** Moral realism = the conjunction of (i) cognitivism — moral sentences express propositions; (ii) success — some of those propositions are true; (iii) mind-independence — their truth does not depend on the attitudes of any actual or idealized observer. The two live secular variants profiled throughout:

- **Robust non-naturalism** — Moore, Ross; contemporary: Parfit, Enoch, Scanlon, Wielenberg. Moral facts are irreducibly normative, not identical to any natural fact.
- **Naturalist realism** — Cornell realism (Boyd, Sturgeon, Brink); reductive forms (Railton). Moral facts are natural facts, knowable a posteriori.

Historically the strain also lived inside full cosmologies — Plato's Form of the Good, the Stoic logos, natural-law theism, Kant's postulates. These are treated in §3 as *bundled* variants, because their treatment is the most interesting finding of the exercise.

## 1. Profile across the twelve primitives

### 1.1 `ultimate_reality` — variant-dependent; the strain's signature primitive

Shared core: moral truths obtain independently of what anyone believes, feels, or would agree to. Whether that is a claim about *what is fundamentally real* splits the strain:

- **Robust non-naturalism** makes an explicit fundamentality claim: reality includes irreducibly normative facts — Enoch's "irreducibly normative truths," Moore's non-natural goodness. Reality outruns the natural.
- **Naturalist realism** adds nothing: moral facts are natural facts, so the ultimate_reality profile is inherited wholesale from naturalism.
- Internal complication worth preserving (rule 7 analog — do not average): Parfit and Scanlon hold the normative truths exist "in a non-ontological sense" (quietism / relaxed realism) — realists who *deny* their own view is an ultimate_reality commitment. A card would have to mark this contested.

### 1.2 `constituents` — variant-dependent, but the one primitive where both variants assert something

Both variants assert that moral properties and facts **exist** — the shared positive constituents claim, and the exact target of Mackie's argument from queerness (error theory concedes cognitivism and attacks the constituents claim). They differ on kind:

- Robust: non-natural properties/relations — goodness, wrongness, reasons — causally and spatiotemporally unlocated, abstract-object-like.
- Naturalist: moral properties are natural properties, e.g. Boyd's homeostatic property clusters; nothing beyond a naturalist inventory.

### 1.3 `origin` — silent

Moral realism says nothing about how reality, life, or the cosmic order arose. Nearby but distinct (per the origin ambiguity test — record *whose* origin): the modal status of moral truths — necessary, uncreated, "brute" (Wielenberg) — is a claim about certain truths, not about cosmogenesis. The silence here is load-bearing: see §3 on orphaning, since evolution — the actual origin story of our moral faculties — is the engine of Street's debunking argument.

### 1.4 `causality_and_agency` — variant-dependent and load-bearing

The deepest structural split, and the one that determines primitive 12:

- **Robust**: moral facts are causally inert. This is a genuine negative causal claim with cosmological content — the causal order of the world is normatively blind; nothing happens *because* it is wrong.
- **Naturalist (Cornell)**: moral facts are causally efficacious — Sturgeon's line that Hitler's depravity explains his actions, that injustice explains rebellion; moral properties earn a place in best explanations.

Shared peripheral commitment: agents can be moved by *recognizing* reasons — some minimal reasons-responsive agency — though realists divide on whether this exceeds naturalistic agency.

### 1.5 `cosmic_structure` — silent (modern), structural only in the Platonic ancestor

Contemporary talk of a "normative realm" or "domain" is standardly disowned as non-spatial and non-literal — under interpretation rule 4, metaphor without licensing context is `unclear`, and the licensing is explicitly refused (Parfit). Only the Platonic bundled variant makes a genuine structure claim (the Forms as a distinct order of reality).

### 1.6 `time` — silent, one peripheral note

Nothing on the form or direction of time. Peripheral: moral truths are standardly held to be atemporal and necessary — true at all times in all worlds — which is a claim about the modal status of some truths, not about time itself (the same distinction the time primitive's exclude-list draws for chronology).

### 1.7 `human_place` — characteristic; implied across all variants

The strain's least advertised but most consistent cosmological consequence: **humans are discoverers of value, not its authors.** We stand under a normative order we did not make and cannot repeal — the direct contrast class is constructivism and expressivism, where valuing agents (or their idealized procedures) are the source. Corollaries:

- The moral order is not anthropocentric in principle: it would bind any rational agent, and moral truths would remain true in our absence.
- Moral standing is grounded in features beings actually have (rationality, sentience), not conferred by convention.

### 1.8 `self_and_consciousness` — silent

No commitments on the metaphysics of mind or personal identity. Peripheral pressure only: the robust variant's epistemology presupposes a faculty capable of non-empirical normative apprehension, which historically nudged intuitionists toward rationalist philosophy of mind — but contemporary robust realists deliberately avoid the commitment.

### 1.9 `fundamental_problem` — silent, with a precondition worth recording

Metaethics diagnoses no pervasive disorder. Two notes:

- Realism supplies the **metaphysical precondition** for any objective fundamental_problem claim: only if moral facts are stance-independent can the world itself be *morally* amiss, rather than merely disapproved of. Realism makes genuine, collective moral error possible (an entire society can simply be wrong).
- At the meta-level the position is characteristically *motivated* as a rescue from nihilism — Parfit's remark that if nihilism were true his life's work would have been wasted; Enoch's "taking morality seriously." But a motivation for holding a doctrine is not a doctrine (the analog of excluding the evaluator's stake under rule-9 discipline), so this stays out of the profile proper.

### 1.10 `transformation` — silent, one peripheral implication

No soteriology and no mechanism. Peripheral: realism underwrites **moral progress as discovery** — the abolition of slavery can be *learning*, a correction toward stance-independent fact, not merely a shift in preferences. Anti-realists must reconstruct progress-talk; realists get it for free. But this is an implication about the status of change, not a proposed path of transformation.

### 1.11 `destiny` — silent, with a historically documented pressure point

No eschatology, no cosmic trajectory. The pressure point: Sidgwick's "dualism of practical reason" — without some cosmic ordering that reconciles duty and self-interest, practical reason may end in irresolvable conflict; Kant postulated God and immortality precisely to make the highest good attainable. Bundled variants answered the pressure with destiny claims. Modern secular realism refuses it and accepts the consequence: **an objective normative order that nothing in the cosmos enforces**. Wrongness carries no cosmic penalty. That refusal is itself a distinctive, if negative, position worth a card claim.

### 1.12 `epistemic_access` — variant-dependent, explicit, the most contested front

- **Robust**: self-evidence and rational intuition (Ross), reflective equilibrium, a priori reasoning. Because primitive 4 made the facts causally inert, this variant faces the Benacerraf–Field reliability challenge and Street's Darwinian dilemma head-on: with no causal contact, why would faculties shaped by evolution track the moral truths? (Third-factor replies: Enoch, Wielenberg.)
- **Naturalist**: a posteriori and broadly empirical — moral knowledge is continuous with scientific knowledge because moral properties do causal-explanatory work.

The coupling is structural: **the position taken on primitive 4 fixes the burden carried on primitive 12.** Causal inertness forces non-empirical access and invites debunking; causal efficacy permits empirical access but must survive Moore's open-question pressure on whether the natural property found is really *wrongness*.

## 2. Profile summary

| Primitive | Status | One-line content |
|---|---|---|
| ultimate_reality | variant-dependent | robust: irreducibly normative facts are part of reality; naturalist: no addition |
| constituents | variant-dependent (shared existence claim) | moral properties exist — non-natural vs. natural kinds |
| origin | silent | — |
| causality_and_agency | variant-dependent, load-bearing | causally inert vs. causally efficacious moral facts |
| cosmic_structure | silent | "normative realm" disowned as metaphor |
| time | silent | (peripheral: moral truths atemporal/necessary) |
| human_place | characteristic | humans discover value; the order binds any rational agent |
| self_and_consciousness | silent | (peripheral: faculty for a priori normative apprehension) |
| fundamental_problem | silent | (precondition: makes objective moral error possible) |
| transformation | silent | (peripheral: moral progress as discovery) |
| destiny | silent | refuses cosmic enforcement of norms (contra Sidgwick/Kant pressure) |
| epistemic_access | variant-dependent, explicit | intuition/reflective equilibrium vs. empirical inquiry |

Substantive commitments on 5 of 12 primitives; a coverage analog of roughly 0.42 — **moral realism is a partial cosmology**, in the same way (discovered in the Yudkowsky dry run) that scientific naturalism's card is partial on the soteriological primitives. Of those 5, the two variants diverge on 4. Consequences for the card deck:

- Under the decision-log rule "broad religions are navigation categories, not single doctrinal vectors," **"moral realism" is a navigation category.** A single moral-realism card would average incompatible positions (violating the spirit of interpretation rule 7).
- If represented at all, it needs **two scoped cards**: *robust non-naturalist realism (Moore–Ross–Parfit–Enoch lineage)* and *Cornell naturalist realism (Boyd–Sturgeon–Brink)*. Candidate provenance for D2-001: SEP "Moral Realism" (Sayre-McCord), "Moral Non-Naturalism" (Ridge), "Moral Naturalism," "Moral Anti-Realism" (Joyce) — URLs and passages to be verified at curation time, not asserted here.

## 3. Findings

### F1. The cosmology/values boundary survives its hardest test — via a subtlety

Moral realism is, in effect, *the thesis that the framework's cosmology/values boundary is not metaphysically deep*: it says values are part of the furniture of reality. Yet the framework represents it without strain — because the realist's meta-claim ("goodness is a stance-independent property") files under `ultimate_reality`/`constituents`/`epistemic_access`, while first-order value content ("equality is good") stays in the values layer, exactly as the boundary table prescribes. The boundary is operational (where claims are filed), and that is what lets it stay neutral between realism and anti-realism.

### F2. Proposed interpretation rule: first-order moral assertion is not evidence of moral-realist cosmology

The practical payoff for extraction. A text dense with confident moral claims ("slavery is evil," "cruelty is always wrong") provides **no evidence** for realist `ultimate_reality`/`constituents` claims, because quasi-realist expressivism (Blackburn) reproduces the whole of first-order moral discourse — including "it would be wrong even if we approved of it" in most constructions — without realist metaphysics. Only explicitly *metaethical* spans license extraction: assertions of mind-independence, moral *discovery*/moral *knowledge* framed against possible collective error, comparisons of moral to mathematical or scientific truth. This is the metaethical sibling of interpretation rule 3 (no inference from identity terms) and should be considered as a wording clarification (patch release) to `docs/COSMOLOGY_PRIMITIVES.md` before D2-001 writes any metaethically scoped card.

### F3. Cosmological orphaning — the lens's most generative output on this strain

Historically, moral realism never traveled alone: Plato grounded it in the Form of the Good (structure + ultimate_reality), Stoicism in the logos (causality), natural-law theism in God's creative ordering (origin + destiny + fundamental_problem), Kant in postulated immortality and God (destiny). Modern secular realism is the historically unusual attempt to keep the objective normative order while dropping every supporting primitive — a **cosmologically orphaned** doctrine, silent on origin, structure, destiny.

Seen through the twelve primitives, the central contemporary objection is precisely an orphaning complaint: Street's debunking argument says that once you accept the naturalistic *origin* story for our faculties and the robust variant's *causal* inertness, the *epistemic_access* claim becomes unaccountable. The strain's live debate is whether a partial cosmology of this shape is stable, or whether it must either re-bundle (the theistic moral argument runs exactly this direction: from orphaned realism back to a full cosmology) or deflate (quietism, constructivism). The lens did not merely classify the position; it exposed the debate's structure as a dependency graph over primitives.

### F4. The primitives are not independent for doctrine profiling

Primitive 4 fixes primitive 12's burden (§1.12). For text analysis, uniform primitive weights are a reasonable default; for doctrine profiling, some primitives are structurally upstream of others. No contract change needed now — fit weights only apply to text-vs-card comparison — but D2-001 curation should record such dependencies in card `scope.limitations` rather than flattening them.

### F5. Placement against the current three-card deck (informal)

Relation vocabulary applied doctrine-to-card, outside its frozen text-to-card home — indicative only:

- **scientific-naturalism**: Cornell realism nests inside it — `compatible` across the board. Robust non-naturalism `contradicts` its ultimate_reality claim ("everything is physical") while remaining `compatible` elsewhere. The existing deck can already *distinguish the two variants* — a good sign for card expressiveness.
- **christian-classical-theism**: `compatible`, and complementary in an instructive way — theism supplies exactly the primitives orphaned realism is silent on (origin, destiny, fundamental_problem). The classical moral argument for God's existence is an inference from the orphaned profile to the bundled one.
- **buddhist-madhyamaka**: `tension` on ultimate_reality — stance-independent moral facts with their own nature sit badly with the emptiness of svabhāva. But the two-truths doctrine complicates it: at the conventional level, karma functions as a robustly objective moral order. This re-triggers contract gap #1 from the Yudkowsky dry run (no relation for rival non-exclusive answers) and adds a new stressor: **the relation vocabulary has no way to say "contradicts ultimately, compatible conventionally."** Level-indexed claims may deserve a note in card schema before D2-001 curates any two-truths tradition at scale.

## 4. Limitations

- Written from general philosophical knowledge; no sources fetched or verified in this run. Every named attribution (Enoch, Sturgeon, Street, Sidgwick, etc.) requires provenance before appearing in a card.
- The doctrinal-status scale in §0 is a local instrument of this report, not part of any frozen contract.
- Fit scores were deliberately not computed: `candidate_fit` is defined for text-evidence with stance and strength weights, and no honest mapping from doctrinal status to evidence strength exists yet. Computing a number would have manufactured false precision.
