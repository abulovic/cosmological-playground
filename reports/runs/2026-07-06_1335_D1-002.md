## Result

The cosmology-card schema is now proven against three deliberately contrasting draft cards — scientific naturalism, Christian classical theism, and Indian Madhyamaka Buddhism — all of which validate via a new dependency-free validator. The schema can carry real content: sourced claims with verbatim passages, per-claim contested flags, and absent primitives represented by omission with documented limitations.

## Changes

- `knowledge/cosmologies/cards/scientific-naturalism.json`: 8 claims, 3 sources (SEP Naturalism, Physicalism, Consciousness); 4 primitives deliberately absent (time, fundamental_problem, transformation, destiny).
- `knowledge/cosmologies/cards/christian-classical-theism.json`: 12 claims incl. two `origin` claims (creation; Aquinas's non-demonstrability of a temporal beginning, contested), 5 sources (Nicene Creed, Summa I q.2 and q.46, SEP Divine Simplicity, SEP Eternity); self_and_consciousness deliberately absent pending source verification.
- `knowledge/cosmologies/cards/buddhist-madhyamaka.json`: 9 claims, 3 sources (SEP Nāgārjuna, SEP Two Truths in India, IEP Madhyamaka); 5 primitives absent as inherited pan-Buddhist background out of the school's argued scope.
- `scripts/validate_cards.py`: stdlib-only validator implementing exactly the JSON Schema subset the card schema uses, plus assertive date/uri format checks and card-level referential integrity (claim source_ids must resolve; source ids unique). CLI validates all cards.
- `tests/test_cosmology_cards.py`: 26 tests — corpus acceptance checks (validation, provenance, contested present in every card, absence representable in ≥2 cards, claimed-primitive union covers all 12, drafts stay drafts) and negative fixtures proving the validator rejects malformed cards.
- `knowledge/cosmologies/README.md`: validator usage and absence/contested conventions.
- `.agent/state.json`: D1-002 completed with evidence; reference_knowledge_base promoted 1 → 2 with evidence paths.

## Evidence and limitations

- Evidence: `knowledge/cosmologies/cards/*.json`, `scripts/validate_cards.py`, `tests/test_cosmology_cards.py`.
- Remaining uncertainty: cards are `review.status: draft` and claim no scholarly authority (human review gate). Several claims are marked medium confidence where they are inferences from the cited surveys (e.g., naturalism's origin claim) or where the source presupposes rather than states the doctrine (creed on the fundamental problem). Classical theism's soul doctrine and Madhyamaka's inherited cosmological background are recorded as scope limitations, not claims.
- New risks or decisions: chose a stdlib-only validator over adding a `jsonschema` dependency, consistent with the repo's dependency-free pattern; the validator is guarded by negative-fixture tests. If a future task adopts a real JSON Schema library, the test suite transfers directly. Absence semantics decision: an unclaimed primitive means `unspecified` (contract rule 8) and must be documented in `scope.limitations`.
